Learn more about NIM

The Department of Technology has recently unveiled their newly developed "Neural Interface Module".

Last updated: 1 October 2035





The Neural Interface Module (NIM) is the newest advancement of today's age. Created through a partnership between the Department of Technology and Department of Health, and powered by a betavoltaic battery, this device lets citizens have greater control and automation over their personal electronic devices.

How to use it

To setup your device complete the following:
 - enable BlueTooth on your device you wish to pair
 - tap a sequence of 3 presses, 3 holds, and again three presses on the side of your NIM
 - a prompt should appear on your device, click yes
Your device has now been setup.
After completing the setup process, the device is very easy to use:
 - tap and hold on the capacitive sensor
 - concentrate on what you want to do on your device Your thought should be processed as an action on your deivce.

How it works

The complex yet compact desgin would not be possible without the work of the Department of Technology.

The components of the device include:
 - Antenna
   The antenna allows for near field communication to your electronic device. It also chains connections to other nearby NIM's to increase reach.
 - Battery
   A betavoltaic battery allows for an estimated active running time of 10 years.
 - Sensor array
   An array of diodes capable of infering electrical brain activity, placed on the prefontal-cortex to directly read the "thoughts" of the user.
 - Processor
 - Power and Data lines
 - Capacitive touch surface
