Learn more about NIM

The Department of Technology has recently unveiled their newly developed "Neural Interface Module".

Last updated: 1 October 2035




The Neural Interface Module (NIM) is the newest advancement of today's age.

Created through a partnership between the Department of Technology and Department of Psychology.

Powered by a betavoltaic battery, means that this device lasts a decade.

This device lets the people of our great country have greater control and automation over their lives.

How to use it

To setup your device complete the following:

  1. enable BlueTooth on your electronic device
  2. tap a sequence of 3 presses, 3 holds, and another three presses on the side of your NIM
  3. a prompt will appear on your device, click connect
Your device should now be setup.

To use this device:
  • tap and hold on the capacitive sensor on the side of the device
  • concentrate on what you want to do on your device
Your thoughts should now be processed as an input on your device.

How it works

The components of the device include:

  • Antenna
    • allows for near and far field communication
  • Battery
    • betavoltaic cell allows for decade long run tine
  • Sensor array
    • sensors placed on prefrontal cortex
  • Processor
    • turns brain activity into digital signals
  • Power and Data lines
    • moves power and information between sensor and main unit
  • Capacitive touch surface
    • tells the device when to activate

How to get yours today

Complete the following:

  • Log in to this site using your governmental login and password
  • On the home screen click on "Get your NIM today"
  • Book in the time and place of your appointment

Frequently asked questions

Q: Is it safe?

A: Yes, we have had the safety of the NIM peer-reviewed, and it is 100% safe (sample size of 2).


Q: How much does this cost?

A: We want the people of our country to live the best they can. So we are providing this device free of charge.


Q: What is the response time of the device?

A: The device has a maximum measured response time of 1ms.


Q: Can I use this device if I have difficulty with my hands?

A: Yes, this device will improve the life of those who struggle with using computers.
